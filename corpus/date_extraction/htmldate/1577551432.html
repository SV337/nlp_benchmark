Second suspect in shooting turns himself in

 
Tatiana Parafiniuk-Talesnick @TatianaSophiaPT

Monday

Apr 13, 2020 at 4:38 PM


A second suspect in a shooting last week in Eugene’s West University neighborhood turned himself in Monday.


The man turned himself in at 1:36 p.m. at Eugene Police Department headquarters and was charged with attempted murder, robbery in the first degree, and robbery in the second degree, according to a Eugene police press release. The man is being held in the Lane County Jail.


Last week, police arrested Demario Aundre Jackson, 30, in connection with an April 8 shooting in the 400 block of East 15th Avenue in Eugene. One person was injured and hospitalized.


Jackson, who was arrested in Springfield the day after the shooting, subsequently was charged with three counts of first-degree robbery with a firearm, one count of second-degree robbery, attempted murder in the second degree, first degree assault and felon in possession of a firearm.
