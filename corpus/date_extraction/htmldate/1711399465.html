Caeneus charges up its exploration tool kit at Mallina

Matt BirneySponsored
Tue, 15 September 2020 1:43PM
Matt Birney

Caeneus Minerals is moving ahead at a rapid rate of knots at its Mallina project in the Pilbara, with detailed exploration now underway across the company’s extensive tenure north of De Grey Mining’s Hemi gold discovery in WA. Caeneus’ rapid gear change comes in the wake of the grant of its tenure in recent weeks and De Grey’s continuing tour-de-force in the region, which has included a massively oversubscribed $100 million capital raising completed this week.

Caeneus’ Mallina project lies 50km south-west of Port Hedland and covers over 475 square kilometres of one of Australia’s most sought after exploration addresses - the sizzling Pilbara goldfield.

The company’s Roberts Hill and Mt Berghaus tenements lie immediately to the north of De Grey’s 2.2-million-ounce Hemi gold discovery with the rapidly expanding discovery pushing out a seemingly endless run of high-grade drill hits as De Grey marches towards its highly anticipated resource upgrade later this year – many believe the new resource will elevate the discovery to ‘world-class’ status.

De Grey’s Hemi discovery lies only 6km south of Caeneus’ tenement boundary – its Shaggy prospect is a mere stone’s throw, or 200m south, of Caeneus’ tenement line.

Caeneus is wasting no time getting in on the act, with newly appointed CEO Rob Mosig rapidly getting exploration underway. The company already has boots on the ground and has completed its first pass reconnaissance work which it quickly followed up with an ultra-detailed, low-level magnetic survey across both the Roberts Hill and Mt Berghaus tenements.

Caeneus also topped up its coffers last week and now has $2 million in the war chest to fund ongoing exploration.

…the placement completed by the Company last week has provided an excellent treasury to conduct meaningful exploration on our Mallina Province Projects. It is with great excitement that we can now fast-track activities at our Roberts Hill project which immediately abuts the De Grey Mining Limited tenements containing their exciting Hemi and Shaggy deposits.

We look forward with much confidence to interpret the latest low-level magnetic data as a prelude to the aircore drilling we expect to start later this year.

Caeneus’ Minerals Chief Executive Officer, Rob Mosig

The Roberts Hill tenure is largely covered by a veneer of sand and gravel which hides the underlying geology and gold mineralisation, forcing the explorer to take a more innovative approach to making discoveries. Whilst this scenario presents a challenge, it also presents a great opportunity, as the likes of De Grey and Novo Resources have already shown through the Pilbara.

An initial evaluation of the recently completed, ultra-detailed magnetics has already begun to define targets across Roberts Hill. Caeneus will use a combination of the recent magnetic geophysical survey and its own unique regional knowledge to delineate potential ‘Hemi-style’ intrusive and shear hosted gold targets for drill testing across the project area.

The company’s initial look at the Roberts Hill Project geophysical data has prompted Caeneus to adopt the same approach to its enigmatic Yule tenure, immediately south of Port Hedland.

The company has initiated an almost identical, ultra-detailed magnetic survey over Yule, optimising the orientation of the flight lines to test the extensions of the regional mineralised Sholl Shear Zone. Exploration across Yule has proven inconclusive and Caeneus believes the added layer of data will help in the interpretation of results from previous geochemical sampling.

With $2 million in the tin, targeting underway and approvals for drilling being lodged with the WA Mines Department, Caeneus is looking to stamp its mark on the Pilbara, initially with aircore drilling at Roberts Hill, before the close of 2020.
