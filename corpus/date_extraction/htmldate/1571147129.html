    Odisha reports first Covid-19 death: 72-year-old man from Bhubaneswar dies

    Ashok Pradhan | TNN | Apr 7, 2020, 15:43 IST
    Representative image.
    BHUBANESWAR: A 72-year-old man from Bhubaneswar who had tested positive for Covid-19 died on Monday, the health department said on Tuesday. He is the first man from Odisha to have died of the disease.
    “One 72 years male belonging to Jharpada had been admitted to AIIMS Bhubaneswar on 4th April with complaint of respiratory distress. He had history of chronic hypertension. He expired on 6th April. He was tested positive for COVID-19. Contact tracing and containment is ongoing,” the health department twitted.
    AIIMS director Gitanjali Batmanabane said the man was kept in an isolation facility of AIIMS. Condition of another 60-year-old man, native of Suryanagar area of Bhubaneswar who has tested positive for Covid-19, admitted in AIIMS is stable, the director said.
    Also, another 45-years-old man from Madhusudan Nagar, Unit IV, Bhubaneswar tested positive for Covid-19, raising total number of positive cases in Odisha to 42 and those in Bhubaneswar to 34.
